Promotional Campaign Analysis — AtliQ Mart

Power BI project analyzing festive-season promotional performance across 50+ South Indian supermarkets to guide future campaign strategy.

Business Problem

AtliQ Mart, a retail chain with 50+ supermarkets across South India, ran large-scale promotional campaigns during Diwali 2023 and Sankranti 2024 on its AtliQ-branded products. The Sales Director needed to know which promotions actually worked — by store, by promo type, and by category — to plan sharper, higher-ROI campaigns for the next festive season instead of repeating what didn't move the needle.

Key Findings


₹7M+ revenue opportunity identified by reallocating spend toward the promo types and categories that consistently outperformed


₹91.1M in cashback/promo-driven revenue tracked and attributed back to specific promotion types and categories


Flagged underperforming stores where promotions failed to lift product movement (Incremental Sold Units) — despite revenue looking fine on the surface


Identified which promo types drive units vs which drive revenue — a distinction that changes how each should be used going forward
Analysis Structure

Built in three layers, moving from "where" to "what kind of promo" to "which product":

1. Store Performance Analysis

City-wise store distribution, revenue before vs after promo, top 10 stores by Incremental Revenue (IR), bottom stores by Incremental Sold Units (ISU), and category performance across the top 3 cities. Pinpoints which stores and cities respond strongly to promotions — and which don't, despite the same campaign running.

2. Promo Type Analysis

Compares every promotion type side-by-side on ISU and IR to separate the promo types driving genuine unit growth from the ones just inflating revenue. Includes a before/after quantity comparison by category and promo type (QSB vs QAP), plus an interactive switch visual to instantly flip between top and bottom 2 promo types by ISU or IR.

3. Category & Product Analysis

Drills into which categories and individual products actually responded to promotions vs which underperformed, with a switch visual for one-at-a-time product comparison and a top-3-categories-by-ISU view for quick prioritization.

Tools & Techniques


Power BI — data modeling and report design across store, promo-type, and product layers


DAX — calculated columns and measures for IR, ISU, and before/after promo comparisons


AI-assisted development — used ChatGPT to help build the Promo View Selector and Product Switch Selector (interactive switch visuals), and AI-assisted DAX for scenario-specific calculated columns



Why This Matters for the Business


Store performance view → where to concentrate promo spend next season, and which underperforming stores need root-cause investigation


Promo type view → which promo formats to scale up (unit-drivers) vs which to use sparingly (revenue-only, low unit lift)


Category/product view → which products deserve featured promo slots vs which are dead weight in a campaign
